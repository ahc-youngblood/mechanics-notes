1 A rod of length \(L\) with quadratic linear mass density \(\lambda(x) = c_0x^2\) is resting on a flat, frictionless and is pinned to that surface through its center of mass.

1.1 In terms of \(L\), find an expression for the distance of the center of mass of the stick from the nearest end.

To find the location of the center of mass we will need the total mass in terms of \(L\) and \(c_0\) so we must integrate the given \(\lambda(x)\)

\begin{align*} M=\int_0^L c_0x^2 dx &= c_0\frac{L^3}{3}\\ \end{align*}

Now we can find

\begin{align*} x_{cm} &= \frac{1}{M}\int_0^L xc_0x^2 dx\\ &= \frac{1}{M}c_0\frac{L^4}{4}\\ &= \frac{3}{4}L \end{align*}

The problem asks for the distance from the nearest end, which is \(\frac{L}{4}\).

1.2 A small mass \(m\) traveling at speed \(v\) collides with and attaches to the stick at the end furthest from the center of mass. Find

1.2.1 The final moment of inertia of the stick + mass.

We will need the moment of inertia of the nonuniform stick so we begin with that. Ultimately we will need the moment of inertia about an axis through the center of mass, but it is easier to compute it about one end (we will choose the less dense end) and then apply the parallel axis theorem.

\begin{align*} I_{end} &= \int_0^L x^2c_0x^2 dx\\ &= c_0\frac{L^5}{5}\\ \end{align*}

Now apply the parallel axis theorem

\begin{align*} I_{end} &= I_{CM} + Md^2\\ I_{CM} &= I_{end} - Md^2\\ &= c_0\frac{L^5}{5} - c_0\frac{L^3}{3}\left(\frac{3L}{4}\right)^2\\ &= c_0\frac{L^5}{5} - c_0\frac{9L^5}{48}\\ &= c_0\frac{L^5(48 - 45)}{240}\\ &= \frac{3}{80}ML^2 \end{align*}

The momentof inertia of the small mass about the center of mass of the stick is \(\frac{9}{16}mL^2\), so the total moment of inertia is

\begin{align*} I_{tot} &= \frac{3}{80}ML^2 + \frac{9}{16}mL^2 \end{align*}

1.2.2 The final angular speed of the stick + mass.

\begin{align*} I_i\omega_i = m\frac{3L}{4}v &= I_{tot}\omega_f\\ \frac{mv}{\frac{1}{20}ML + \frac{3}{4}mL} &= \omega_f \end{align*}

2 A person uses a shovel of mass \(m\) to lift a load of mass \(M\). Assume that the mass of the shovel is uniformly distributed. Also assume that this is a lifting phase so that the handle of the shovel (the point furthest from the load) held in one of the person's hands is acting as the fulcrum (pivot) of the system.

2.1 If the person's other hand is \(7L/8\) from the handle, find expressions for the forces exerted by each of their hands to hold the system in static equilibrium in a horizontal position.

Let us call the force due to the hand on the handle \(F_2\) and the force due to the other hand \(F_2\). Treating the handle as the pivot we have the torque equation

\begin{align*} (F_1)\left(\frac{7L}{8}\right) - mg\frac{L}{2} - MgL &= 0\\ F_1 &= \frac{mg\frac{L}{2} + MgL}{\left(\frac{7L}{8}\right)}\\ &= \frac{1}{7}(4mg + 8Mg) \end{align*}

The force equation will be

\begin{align*} F_2 + F_1 - mg - Mg &= 0\\ F_2 &= mg + Mg - F_1\\ F_2 &= \frac{1}{7}(3mg - Mg) \end{align*}

2.2 Repeat part (a) for the case where the person's other hand is instead \(L/4\) from the handle.

Only one constant coefficient is changed compared to part (a)

\begin{align*} (F_1)\left(\frac{L}{4}\right) - mg\frac{L}{2} - MgL &= 0\\ F_1 &= \frac{mg\frac{L}{2} + MgL}{\left(\frac{L}{4}\right)}\\ &= (2mg + 4Mg) \end{align*}

The force equation will be

\begin{align*} F_2 + F_1 - mg - Mg &= 0\\ F_2 &= mg + Mg - F_1\\ F_2 &= -(mg + 3Mg) \end{align*}

3.1 Neptune has been observed to have an orbital period of 165 (Earth) years. By what factor is Neptune's orbital radius greater than Earth's?

From Kepler's third law we know that

\begin{align*} \frac{T_N^2}{T_E^2} &= \frac{R_N^3}{R_E^3}\\ 165^2 = (R_N/R_E)^3\\ ^3\sqrt{27225}R_E &= R_N\\ 30.1R_E &\approx R_N \end{align*}

3.2 One of Neptune's moons, Triton, orbits the planet at an average orbital radius of 354,800 km with an orbital velocity of 4.4 km/s. What is Neptune's mass?

We equate the centripetal force needed to keep this moon in orbit to the gravitational force acting on it.

\begin{align} m\frac{v^2}{r} &= \frac{GM_Nm}{r^2}\\ \frac{v^2r}{G} &= M_N\\ \frac{(4.4\times 10^3 \mathrm{m/s})^2(3.548\times 10^8\mathrm{m})}{\left(6.674\times 10^{-11} \frac{\mathrm{N}\cdot \mathrm{m}^2}{\mathrm{kg}^2}\right)} &= M_N\\ 1.025\times 10^{26} \mathrm{kg} &\approx M_N \end{align}
