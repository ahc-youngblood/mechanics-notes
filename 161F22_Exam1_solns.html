2 A ball is thrown at an angle \(\theta\) up to the top of a cliff of height \(l\) from a point a distance \(l\) from the base of the cliff. Find the initial speed \(v_i\) (in terms of \(\theta\)) for the ball to just hit the edge of the cliff.

From horizontal and vertical displacement with \(t\) being the time taken to reach the edge of the cliff:

\begin{align*} l &= (v_i\cos\theta)t\\ l &= (v_i\sin\theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2\\ l &= l\tan\theta - \frac{gl^2}{2v_i^2\cos^2\theta}\\ v_i &= \sqrt{\frac{gL}{2\cos\theta(\sin\theta - \cos\theta)}} \end{align*}

3 An inclined plane of mass \(M\) and angle \(\theta\) is sitting on the floor of an elevator. A block of mass \(m\) is released from rest (relative to the incline) from the top of the incline (which is a height \(h\) above the floor of the elevator).

3.1 If the incline is frictionless and the elevator is accelerating downward with an acceleration of magnitude \(a_d\), find an expression for the time it will take the block to reach the bottom of the incline.

If there were no incline, the magnitude of the acceleration down the incline would be \(g-a_d\), since the incline is present the acceleration parallel to the incline is \(a_{||}=-(g-a_d)\) where we treat up the incline as the positive direction. The displacement, with the same sign convention will be \(\Delta x_{||} = -\frac{h}{\sin\theta}\) so

\begin{align*} -\frac{h}{\sin\theta} &= -\frac{1}{2}(g-a_d)\sin\theta t^2\\ t &= \sqrt{\frac{2h}{(g-a_d)\sin^2\theta}} \end{align*}

3.2 If the coefficient of static friction between the incline and the block in \(\mu_s\), find an expression for the magnitude \(a_u\) of the minimum upward acceleration required to keep the block from sliding down the incline.

Following the same logic as the previous part, the force equation parallel to the incline (for the maximum possible static friction) is:

\begin{align*} -m(g+a_u)\sin\theta + \mu_sm(g+a_u)\cos\theta = 0\\ \tan\theta &= \mu_s \end{align*}

Note that the value of \(a_u\) does not matter, just as the mass and the value of \(g\) (as long as they are not 0) do not matter.

3.3 Find expressions for

3.3.1 The force of the floor of the elevator on the incline in case 1.1.

\begin{align*} (M+m)(g-a_d) \end{align*}

3.3.2 The force of the floor of the elevator on the incline in case 1.2.

\begin{align*} (M+m)(g+a_d) \end{align*}

4 The figure shows a mass \(m_1\) on a plane incline at an angle \(\theta\) connected by a massless, unstretchable cord over a massless, frictionless pulley to a second mass \(m_2\). The coefficient of static friction is \(\mu_s\).

4.1 Draw and label a free body diagram for \(m_1\) in the absence of friction.

4.2 For \(\mu_s = 0, \mu_k \neq 0\), find an expression for the acceleration of \(m_1\)

\begin{align*} m_1a &= T-m_1g\sin\theta - \mu_km_1g\cos\theta\\ m_2a &= m_2g - T\\ a &= g\frac{m_2 - m_1\sin\theta -\mu_km_1\cos\theta}{m_1+m_2} \end{align*}

4.3 For \(\mu_s \neq 0\), find an expression for the minimum \(m_2\) that will keep \(m_1\) from sliding down the incline.

\begin{align*} 0 &= m_2g + \mu_sm_1g\cos\theta - m_1g\sin\theta\\ m_2 &= m_1(\sin\theta - \mu_s\cos\theta)\\ \end{align*}

4.4 For \(\mu_s \neq 0\), find an expression for the minimum \(m_2\) that will cause \(m_1\) to slide up the incline.

In this case the sign of the friction force is reversed so \(m_2 = m_1(\sin\theta + \mu_s\cos\theta)\).
