6 Refer to the diagram above. Assume \(m_A\) and \(m_B\) are separated by a distance \(s\).

6.1 If \(m_A, m_B, m_C, m_D\) are the same. What is the distance of the center of mass of the set of masses from the origin?

\(\vec{x}_A = -s\hat{i} + s\hat{j}\)

\(\vec{x}_B = s\hat{i} + s\hat{j}\)

\(\vec{x}_C = s\hat{i} - s\hat{j}\)

\(\vec{x}_D = -s\hat{i} - s\hat{j}\)

Now we can apply the formula for the center of mass, in this case of four objects \[ \vec{x}_{cm} = \frac{m_A\vec{x}_A + m_B\vec{x}_B + m_C\vec{x}_C + m_D\vec{x}_D}{m_A+m_B+m_C+m_D} \] Since the vectors cancel when summed together we can say that the center of mass is at the origin or \(\vec{O}\) and therefore the distance from the origin, which would be \(|\vec{x}_{cm}|\) is 0m.

6.2 If \(m_A\) has twice the mass of any of the other masses, which all have the same mass as each other. What is the new distance of the center of mass from the origin \(O\) in terms of \(s\)?

Now \(2m\vec{x}_A + m\vec{x}_B + m\vec{x}_C + m\vec{x}_D = m\vec{x}_A = -ms\hat{i} + ms\hat{j}\).

Also, \(m_A+m_B+m_C+m_D\) = \(5m\) so \(\vec{x}_{cm} = \frac{1}{5}(-s\hat{i} + s\hat{j})\) and the distance is \(\frac{\sqrt{2}s}{5}\).

6.3 If instead \(m_A\) is removed, what is the new distance of the center of mass from the origin \(O\)?

In this case \(m\vec{x}_B + m\vec{x}_C + m\vec{x}_D = ms\hat{i} - ms\hat{j}\). This time the total mass is just \(3m\) so \(\vec{x}_{cm} = \frac{1}{3}(s\hat{i} - s\hat{j})\) and the distance will be \(\frac{\sqrt{2}s}{3}\).

6.4 If both \(m_A\) and \(m_C\) are removed and \(m_D=3m_B\), what is the new distance of the cneter of mass from the origin \(O\)?.

For this case \(m_B\vec{x}_B + 3m_B\vec{x}_D = -2m_Bs\hat{i} - 2m_Bs\hat{j}\). The total mass is \(4m_B\) so the position vector for the center of mass is \(\vec{x}_{cm} = \frac{1}{2}(-s\hat{i} - s\hat{j})\). The distance will be \(\frac{\sqrt{2}s}{2}\).

11 Consider two displacement vectors, \(\vec{x}_1\) with \(|\vec{x}_1|\) = 7m and \(\vec{x}_2\) with \(|\vec{x}_2|\) = 4m.

11.1 What is the magnitude of the vector with the greatest magnitude that a sum of these two vectors can form?

The greatest magnitude occurs when the two vectors in the sum are along the same axis and point in the same direction. In this case \(|\vec{x}_1 + \vec{x}_2| = |\vec{x}_1| + |\vec{x}_2|\) = 7m + 4m = 11m.

11.2 What is the magnitude of the vector with the least magnitude that a sum of these two vectors can form?

The least magnitude occurs when the two vectors in the sum are along the same axis and point in opposite directions. In this case \(|\vec{x}_1 + \vec{x}_2| = |\vec{x}_1| - |\vec{x}_2|\) = 7m - 4m = 3m.
