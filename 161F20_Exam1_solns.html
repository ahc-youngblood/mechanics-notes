1 The figure on the right (on the exam) represents a free body diagram corresponding to three massless cords under tension pulling on a small mass. Assume that any gravitational force is negligible. Assume that the system is in equilibrium.

1.1 With respect to our standard coordinate system (\(\hat{i}\) positive to the right and \(\hat{j}\) positive upward), find the scalar components \(T_{Ax},T_{Ay},T_{Bx},T_{By},T_{Cx},T_{Cy}\) in terms of the magnitudes \(T_A,T_B,T_C\) and the angle \(\theta_C\).

\begin{align*} T_{Ax} &= -T_A\\ T_{Ay} &= 0\\ T_{Bx} &= 0\\ T_{By} &= -T_B\\ T_{Cx} &= T_C\cos\theta_C\\ T_{Cy} &= T_C\sin\theta_C \end{align*}

1.2 Find the Cartesian (\(\hat{i}\) and \(\hat{j}\) with respect to the same coordinate system) components, magnitude, and angle \(\theta_C\) of the vector \(\vec{T}_C\) in terms of the magnitudes of \(\vec{T}_A\) and \(\vec{T}_B\).

\begin{align*} \vec{T}_C &= T_A\hat{i} + T_B\hat{j}\\ T_C &= \sqrt{T_A^2 + T_B^2}\\ \theta_C &= \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{T_B}{T_A}\right) \end{align*}

1.3 Find numerical values for part 1.2 if \(T_A\) = 8N and \(T_B\) = 17N.

\begin{align*} \vec{T}_C &= 8\mathrm{N}{i} + 17\mathrm{N}\hat{j}\\ T_C &= \sqrt{(8\mathrm{N})^2 + (17\mathrm{N})^2} = 66N \\ \theta_C &= \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{17\mathrm{N}}{8\mathrm{N}}\right) = 65^\circ \end{align*}

2 Think of the starter of an outboard motor as a cord wrapped around a solid cylinder (\(I_{cyl} = \frac{1}{2}MR^2\)) of radius \(R_c\). It takes time \(\Delta t\) for the cylinder to reach and angular velocity \(\omega\) when the cord is pulled on by a constant force of magnitude \(F\).

2.1 Find an expression for the mass \(m_c\) of the cylinder in terms of \(R_c\), \(\Delta t\), \(\omega\), and \(F\).

\begin{align*} R_cF &= I\alpha\\ \frac{R_cF}{I} &= \alpha\\ \frac{2F}{m_cR_c} &= \alpha\\ \end{align*}

Also, \(\omega = \alpha\Delta t \Rightarrow \alpha = \frac{\omega}{\Delta t}\). So

\begin{align*} \frac{2F}{m_cR_c} &= frac{\omega}{\Delta t}\\ \frac{2F\Delta t}{\omega R_c} &= m_c \end{align*}

2.2 Find a numerical value for \(m_c\) if $R_c = 7.00$cm, \(\Delta t\) = 2.00 s, \(\omega\) = 500.0 rad/s, and $F = 50.0$N.

$m_c ≈ 2.86$kg

3 A ball is thrown at an angle \(\theta\) up to the top of a cliff of height \(l\) from a point a distance \(l\) from the base of the cliff. Find the initial speed \(v_i\) (in terms of \(\theta\)) for the ball to just hit the edge of the cliff.

4 The figure shows a mass \(m_1\) on a plane incline at an angle \(\theta\) connected by a massless, unstretchable cord over a massless, frictionless pulley to a second mass \(m_2\). The coefficient of static friction is \(\mu_s\).

4.1 Draw and label a free body diagram for \(m_1\) in the absence of friction.

4.2 For \(\mu_s = 0, \mu_k \neq 0\), find an expression for the acceleration of \(m_1\)

\begin{align*} m_1a &= T-m_1g\sin\theta - \mu_km_1g\cos\theta\\ m_2a &= m_2g - T\\ a &= g\frac{m_2 - m_1\sin\theta -\mu_km_1\cos\theta}{m_1+m_2} \end{align*}

4.3 For \(\mu_s \neq 0\), find an expression for the minimum \(m_2\) that will keep \(m_1\) from sliding down the incline.

\begin{align*} 0 &= m_2g + \mu_sm_1g\cos\theta - m_1g\sin\theta\\ m_2 &= m_1(\sin\theta - \mu_s\cos\theta)\\ \end{align*}

4.4 For \(\mu_s \neq 0\), find an expression for the minimum \(m_2\) that will cause \(m_1\) to slide up the incline.

In this case the sign of the friction force is reversed so \(m_2 = m_1(\sin\theta + \mu_s\cos\theta)\).

4.5 If $μ_s = 0.5, μ_k = 0.25, θ = 30^ˆ, m_1 = 200$g and \(m_2\) is the minimum mass from part (d), find the numerical value of the tension in the cord.

\begin{align*} T &= m_1g(\sin\theta + \mu_s\cos\theta) \end{align*}
